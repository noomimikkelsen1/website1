Melanoidin Technology

The chemistry the world overlooked, engineered into a platform.

Melanoidins are among nature's most abundant — and least understood — biopolymers. We've built the science, the process, and the proof to turn them into multifunctional ingredients.

What they are

Born of the Maillard reaction.

Melanoidins are high-molecular-weight biopolymers formed when sugars and amino acids react under heat — the Maillard reaction. It's the chemistry behind roasted coffee, baked bread, and seared food.

Their complex, polyphenol-rich structure gives them remarkable bioactivity: antioxidant, antimicrobial, photoprotective, and anti-glycation properties that conventional ingredients struggle to match.

Maillard biopolymer
Functional properties

Multifunctional by molecular design.

Antioxidant

Neutralises free radicals; protects cells and formulations from oxidative stress.

Matrix-protective

Neutralises reactive species that erode collagen and elastin.

Anti-glycation

Protects collagen and proteins from sugar-induced damage and ageing.

Hydrating

Humectant polysaccharides bind water and reduce transepidermal loss.

SPF-boosting

Optimises UV-filter film formation for higher measured SPF from lower filter loads.
